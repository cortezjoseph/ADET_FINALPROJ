Jute Mallow (Saluyot)

Scientific Name: Corchorus Olitorius

Plant Description

Jute mallow, also known as Nalta jute, Jew's mallow, or Corchorus olitorius, is a leafy green vegetable that is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is related to okra and hibiscus. Jute mallow is primarily grown for its nutritious leaves, which are commonly used as a food source in various cuisines.aluyot can be found anywhere in the Philippines being hardy and requiring little maintenance. It grows abundantly in farmed environments as well as in the wild

Edible Parts

Leaves and tender stems are eaten boiled, stew, stir-fried, or in soup. Sticky leaf mass is used as vegetarian spread.

How to Use

Saluyot is generally cooked as stew, creating a thick syrup comparable in consistency to okra normally taken with rice or other staple.You can enjoy jute leaves by adding them to stews and stir-fries. Alternatively, you can also enjoy them raw, which is just as healthy and delicious, by adding them to smoothies and salads.
