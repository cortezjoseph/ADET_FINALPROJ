Five-Leaved Chaste Tree (Lagundi)

Scientific Name: Vitex Negundo

Plant Description

Vitex negundo, commonly known as the "Five-leaved chaste tree" or "Nirgundi," is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is well-known for its medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine. Additionally it is a shrub that grows in the Philippines. It is one of the ten herbal medicines endorsed by the Philippine Department of Health as an effective herbal medicine with proven therapeutic value.

Edible Parts

The edible parts of Vitex negundo are primarily the leaves and tender shoots of the plant.

How to Use

Lagundi leaves can be grated and steeped in boiling water. It can also be mixed with other herbal medicines. It is recommended to take a cup three times a day to relieve coughing. To relieve cough and effectively manage asthma symptoms, boil at least half a cup of Lagundi leaves in 2 cups of water.
